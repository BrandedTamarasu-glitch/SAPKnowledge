Here are the EXACT field tables and details per org unit (use this content verbatim, do not invent fields):

**Client (MANDT):**
Fields: MANDT (client number, 3-digit: 100, 200, 800), MTEXT (description), CCCATEGORY (client category: P=production, T=test, C=customizing)
Table: T000 | Create/Display: SCC4
Configured here: Currency decimal format, date format, logon settings
Common pattern: 000 (SAP reference), 001 (delivery), 100-899 (customizing/production)
Cross-module impact: All data partitioned by client — users, master data, transactions

**Company Code (BUKRS):**
Fields: BUKRS (4-char: 1000, US01, DE01), BUTXT (company name), ORT01 (city), LAND1 (country: US, DE, GB), WAERS (currency: USD, EUR, GBP), KTOPL (chart of accounts: INT, CAUS), PERIV (fiscal year variant: K4=calendar year)
Table: T001 | Create/Display: OX02
Configured here: Fiscal year variant, chart of accounts, local currency, posting period variant, field status variant, tolerance groups, tax settings
Common pattern: One company code per legal entity; country-based for tax isolation
Cross-module impact: All MM goods movements (MIGO) and SD billing (VF01) from assigned plants/sales orgs generate FI postings in this company code
S/4HANA callout: "No significant structural change. Company code remains the core FI org unit. Universal Journal (ACDOCA) replaces separate FI/CO tables but CC concept is unchanged."

**Plant (WERKS):**
Fields: WERKS (4-char: 1000, US10), NAME1 (plant name), BWKEY (valuation area, usually = plant), FABKL (factory calendar), KUNNR (customer number for intercompany)
Table: T001W | Create/Display: OX10
Configured here: Valuation area, MRP settings, factory calendar, batch management
Common pattern: One plant per physical location or manufacturing site
Cross-module impact: Goods movements in this plant post to the assigned company code's books; valuation area determines inventory value

**Storage Location (LGORT):**
Fields: WERKS (parent plant), LGORT (4-char: 0001, RM01, FG01), LGOBE (description)
Table: T001L | Create/Display: OX09
Configured here: Negative stock allowed (via plant-level config), physical inventory settings
Common pattern: Separate SLocs for raw materials, finished goods, QA, scrap
Cross-module impact: Inventory balances tracked at plant+SLoc level; movements between SLocs are transfer postings (mvt type 311)

**Sales Organization (VKORG):**
Fields: VKORG (4-char: 1000, US10), BUKRS (assigned company code)
Table: TVKO | Create/Display: OVX5
Configured here: Pricing procedures, credit control area assignment, incoterms
Common pattern: One per market region or sales channel
Cross-module impact: SD billing documents (VF01) create FI accounting documents in the assigned company code; revenue and receivables post here

**Distribution Channel (VTWEG):**
Fields: VTWEG (2-char: 10, 20, 30)
Table: TVTW | Create/Display: OVXI
Configured here: Partner determination, pricing conditions
Common pattern: 10=direct sales, 20=wholesale, 30=retail
Cross-module impact: Part of Sales Area — determines which master data (customer, pricing, material determination) is referenced
Note: "Define common distribution channels (OVR1) to share master data across DCs"

**Division (SPART):**
Fields: SPART (2-char: 01, 02)
Table: TSPA | Create/Display: OVXB
Configured here: Material determination, product hierarchy
Common pattern: One per product line (01=product line A, 02=product line B)
Cross-module impact: Part of Sales Area; also used in business area derivation (plant + division)
Note: "Define common divisions (OVR2) to share master data across divisions"

**Sales Area (VKORG + VTWEG + SPART):**
This is NOT a separate org unit — it is the combination of Sales Org + Distribution Channel + Division. Do NOT give it a field table. Instead write:
```markdown
### Sales Area (VKORG + VTWEG + SPART)

Sales Area is the combination of Sales Organization + Distribution Channel + Division. It is not a separate org unit with its own master data table.

**Set up:** OVXG (assign distribution channel and division to sales organization)

All SD master data — customer master (sales area data), pricing conditions, material determination — is maintained per Sales Area. Changing the Sales Area combination changes which master data is referenced in sales documents.

See: `modules/sd/CLAUDE.md` for sales process details
```

**Purchasing Organization (EKORG):**
Fields: EKORG (4-char: 1000, PU01), BUKRS (company code, if company-code-specific)
Table: T024E | Create/Display: OX08
Configured here: Purchasing value limits, tolerance settings
Common pattern: Three assignment modes: (a) company-code-specific (assigned to one CC), (b) cross-plant within one CC, (c) cross-company-code (not assigned to any CC)
Cross-module impact: Purchase orders and invoice verification (MIRO) generate FI postings in the assigned company code
Note about assignment modes: Be explicit about all three modes.

**Purchasing Group (EKGRP):**
Fields: EKGRP (3-char: 001, PG1), EKNAM (name), EKTEL (phone)
Table: T024 | Create/Display: OME4
Configured here: Configured at client level; not assigned to org unit in hierarchy
Common pattern: Represents a buyer or group of buyers; assigned in purchasing documents and material master (purchasing view)
Cross-module impact: Controls authorization for purchasing documents; used in release strategies

**Controlling Area (KOKRS):**
Fields: KOKRS (4-char: 1000, CO01), BEZEI (name), KOINH (CO area currency), KTOPL (chart of accounts)
Table: TKA01 | Create/Display: OKKP
Configured here: Fiscal year variant, operating concern, CO version, cost center standard hierarchy, profit center standard hierarchy
Common pattern: Assignment control option 1 = CO area same as CC (1:1); option 2 = cross-company-code (1:many). All CCs under one CA must share same chart of accounts and fiscal year variant.
Cross-module impact: All cost postings (cost centers, internal orders, profit centers) within assigned CCs managed under this CA; settlements can cross CC boundaries within the same CA
S/4HANA callout: "1:1 (CA = CC) strongly recommended. Company code validation in OKKP is mandatory and cannot be deactivated. Cross-CC allocation technically possible but discouraged. New implementations default to 1:1. S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition enforces single controlling area (A000)."

**Cost Center (KOSTL):**
Fields: KOSTL (10-char: 0000001000), BUKRS (company code), KOSAR (category: production, admin, sales, R&D), VERAK (responsible person), WAERS (currency)
Table: CSKS (texts: CSKT) | Create: KS01 | Display: KS03
Configured here: Category, responsible person, hierarchy area, currency
Common pattern: Organized in standard hierarchy (required, defined in OKEON/KSH1)
Cross-module impact: Receives cost postings from FI (overhead, labor); allocations and assessments distribute costs within the controlling area

**Profit Center (PRCTR):**
Fields: PRCTR (10-char: 0000001000), BUKRS (company code), SEGMENT (for IFRS reporting, if New GL active), VERAK (responsible person)
Table: CEPC (texts: CEPCT) | Create: KE51 | Display: KE53
Configured here: Responsible person, segment assignment, hierarchy area
Common pattern: Organized in standard hierarchy (required, defined in KCH1)
Cross-module impact: Enables profit center accounting for internal P&L; segment is derived from profit center during posting
S/4HANA callout: "Profit Center Accounting integrated into Universal Journal (ACDOCA). Separate PCA ledger (EC-PCA, tables GLPCA/GLPCT) eliminated. Real-time, no reconciliation needed. Profit center is a standard ACDOCA dimension."

**Business Area (GSBER):**
Fields: GSBER (4-char: 1000, BA01)
Table: TGSB (texts: TGSBT) | Create/Display: OX03
Configured here: Cross-company-code; shared at client level
Common pattern: Used for cross-CC balance sheet reporting; derived from plant+division combination or entered manually
Cross-module impact: Derived automatically during SD billing and MM goods movements; enables cross-CC financial statements by business area
S/4HANA callout: "Still functional but further discouraged. SAP recommends profit center + segment for all internal and external reporting. No technical removal, but best practice is to migrate away."
Note: "SAP recommendation: use profit center instead of business area for new implementations."

**Segment (SEGMENT):**
Fields: SEGMENT (10-char)
Table: FAGL_SEGM (texts: FAGL_SEGMT) | Create: via SPRO | Display: SM30 V_FAGL_SEGM
Configured here: Segment code and description only; derived from profit center master data
Common pattern: Used for IFRS 8 / US GAAP ASC 280 segment reporting
Cross-module impact: Derived from profit center during posting — not entered manually on documents; enables segment-level financial statements
S/4HANA callout: "Always available natively — document splitting and segment derivation from profit center work without New GL activation prerequisite. Lower barrier to entry for segment reporting."
CRITICAL NOTE in the section text: "Requires New GL (FAGL) activation in ECC 6 AND document splitting configured. Classic GL does not support segments."

**Functional Area (FKBER):**
Fields: FKBER (4-char: 0100, 0200)
Table: TFKB (texts: TFKBT) | Create: OKBD / FM_FUNCTION | Display: OKBD
Configured here: Functional area code and description
Common pattern: Categories: Production, Administration, Sales & Marketing, R&D; derived from cost center (via substitution) or entered on GL account master
Cross-module impact: Enables cost-of-sales P&L reporting (as opposed to total-cost P&L); niche usage for companies required to report cost-of-sales format
S/4HANA callout: "Still optional but easier to implement — functional area derivation improved in S/4HANA."

**Section 5: Cross-Module Consequences table**

```markdown
## Cross-Module Consequences

| Assignment | Downstream Impact |
|------------|-------------------|
| Plant → Company Code | Goods movements (MIGO) generate FI postings in this CC's books. Valuation area typically = plant. |
| Sales Org → Company Code | SD billing (VF01) creates FI accounting documents. Revenue and receivables post to this CC. |
| Purchasing Org → Company Code | Purchase orders and invoice verification (MIRO) generate FI postings in this CC. |
| Controlling Area → Company Code | All cost postings (cost centers, internal orders, profit centers) managed under this CA. CA must share chart of accounts with all assigned CCs. |
| Cost Center → Controlling Area | Cost postings reported and allocated within this CA. Settlements cross CC boundaries only within same CA. |
| Profit Center → Segment | Segment derived from PC master data during posting. Enables segment-level financial statements (requires New GL + document splitting in ECC 6). |
| Business Area derivation | Derived from plant + division during SD billing and MM goods movements. Enables cross-CC balance sheet by business area. |
| Sales Area combination | Customer master, pricing conditions, material determination all maintained per Sales Area. Changing combination changes referenced master data. |
| Purchasing Org → Plant | Determines which POrg can create POs for which plant. A plant can be serviced by multiple POrgs. |
```

| Profit Center Accounting | Separate PCA ledger (EC-PCA, GLPCA) | Integrated in Universal Journal (ACDOCA) | Real-time, no reconciliation |
| Segment Reporting | Requires New GL + document splitting | Always available natively | Lower barrier to entry |
| Business Area | Functional, used for cross-CC reporting | Further discouraged; use PC + segment | Migration recommended |

| Functional Area | Optional, requires configuration | Still optional but derivation improved | Minor simplification |
